Sushi Adventure

If I had to choose one food to eat for the rest of my life, it would be sushi. Sushi's directly translate to "it's sour." Sushi fundamentally contains rice and seafood. It often also has seaweed and other ingredients like imitation crab and vegetables. Originally, the main focus of sushi was just the the rice; specifically rice with vinegar with some fish on top. In present day, sushi has evolved to be so much more.

  • Nigiri

    • Most people think of this when they hear sushi. It's simply rectangular shaped rice with typically a cut of fish on top. There maybe other types having tamago (sweet egg), crab, or other shellfish on top. A very simple dish that highlights the freshness of the ingredient.

  • Uramaki

    • These are commonly known as sushi rolls. It is various types of ingredients which can include a selected fish, imitation crab, and vegetables that are wrapped around with seaweed. A key point is that the rice is on the outside. From here, there are rolls that add more ingredients on top such as more fish, along with different sauce or sauces.

  • Temaki

    • Hand rolls are very appealing to the eye and are a cone shaped form of sushi. While it may have similar ingredients as the Uramaki, it includes less of them for each hand roll so that the fish inside is emphasized more. Nigiri would be the best form to enjoy purely the fish with rice, but Temaki includes a little more texture and flavor. Also, it's fun it eat!

Food is great. I like a lot of different types of food, but sushi has a special place in my stomach. I've started from the humble California roll and have reached up to Uni Gunkan. With each level up, the prices soar to heights unimaginable. I've only scratched the surface of the sushi world and hope to explore more of it in the future. With the limits of my wallet, I will continue to enjoy the paradise of sushi.
